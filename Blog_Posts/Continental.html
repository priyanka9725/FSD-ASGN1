Continental Snacks

Dive into the tender juicy and flavorful Baby Corn Sesame Fry!

Continental snacks trace their origins to the diverse culinary traditions of Europe. From the delicate pastries of France to the savory tapas of Spain, these snacks reflect the continent's rich tapestry of flavors and cultures. Influenced by centuries of trade and cultural exchange, continental snacks incorporate a wide array of ingredients and techniques, ranging from Mediterranean simplicity to Eastern European complexity. Whether enjoyed as appetizers, light bites, or indulgent treats, continental snacks offer a delicious glimpse into the diverse and vibrant gastronomic heritage of Europe.

INGREDIENTS

  • 200g baby corn, washed and trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds
  • Oil for frying
  • Chopped spring onions for garnish (optional)

    • For Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar or white vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch dissolved in 2 tablespoons water

    • INSTRUCTIONS

    1. In a bowl, mix together the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, ginger-garlic paste, soy sauce, red chili powder, and salt. Add a little water to form a thick batter.
    2. Add the baby corn to the batter and toss until they are well coated.
    3. Heat oil in a frying pan or wok over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the sesame seeds and fry for a few seconds until they start to pop.
    4. Carefully add the coated baby corn to the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry in batches if necessary.
    5. Fry the baby corn until they are golden brown and crispy, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking. Once done, remove them from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
    6. In a separate pan, heat sesame oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
    7. Stir in soy sauce, honey or maple syrup, rice vinegar, and chili flakes (if using). Cook for a minute.
    8. Stir in the cornstarch-water mixture and cook until the sauce thickens slightly.
    9. Add the fried baby corn to the sauce and toss until they are evenly coated.
    10. Garnish with chopped spring onions if desired and serve hot as a delicious appetizer or side dish.
    Bon Appetit!!
