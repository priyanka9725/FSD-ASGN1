Chinese Sizzlers

Experience a tantalizing fusion of sizzle and spice with our Chinese Sizzlers! Get ready for a culinary adventure that will leave you craving more!

Delight in the perfect harmony of spicy and savory flavors, as tender paneer meets a tantalizing blend of aromatic spices and vibrant vegetables. Each bite is a symphony of texture and taste, with the sizzle adding an extra layer of excitement to every mouthful. Served on a sizzling platter, this Paneer Chilli Sizzler is not just a meal, but an experience that ignites the senses. Whether you're a spice aficionado or a curious foodie, this sizzler promises to tantalize your taste buds. Indulge in a fusion of flavors that will leave you wanting to savor every last bite!Bon Appetit!!


INGREDIENTS

  • 250g paneer (cottage cheese), cubed
  • 2 tablespoons cornflour
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
  • 1 medium-sized capsicum (bell pepper), diced
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup 1 tablespoon vinegar 1 teaspoon sugar 1
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper powder 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste 2 tablespoons vegetable oil Spring onions, for garnish
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish (optional)

    • INSTRUCTIONS

    1. In a bowl, mix the cornflour, all-purpose flour, salt, and pepper. Toss the paneer cubes in this mixture until they are well coated.
    2. Heat oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the coated paneer cubes and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove the paneer cubes from the pan and set them aside on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
    3. In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed. Add minced garlic, chopped ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a minute until they release their aroma.
    4. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
    5. Next, add diced capsicum and cook for 2-3 minutes until they are slightly tender but still crisp.
    6. In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, chili sauce, tomato ketchup, vinegar, sugar, red chili powder, and a pinch of salt.
    7. Pour this sauce mixture into the pan and stir well until everything is combined.
    8. Add the fried paneer cubes to the pan and toss them gently with the sauce until they are evenly coated.
    9. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, allowing the paneer to absorb the flavors of the sauce.
    10. Garnish with chopped spring onions and sesame seeds (if using).
    11. Serve hot as an appetizer or as a side dish with fried rice or noodles.
    Bon Appetit!!
